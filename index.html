    Activity Overview

    This 90-minute CPD-certified live symposium features global experts who will explore challenges faced in managing hemophilia A in pediatric and adult patients.

    Area of Focus

    Extended half-life Factor VIII replacement agents and their role in the treatment of hemophilia A with inhibitors, as well as new therapeutic models for treating hemophilia A patients with inhibitors, including bispecific monoclonal antibody therapy.

    Learning Objectives
    Upon completion of this activity, participants will have increased knowledge regarding:
    • Epidemiology of inhibitor development in PWH-A
      • Current approaches to management of PWH-A with inhibitors
    • Burden of inhibitors on PWH-A and their caregivers
    • Emerging therapies for PWH-A and inhibitors
    • Risk factors for inhibitor development in PWH-A
    • Current approaches to management of PWH-A with inhibitors, including extended half-life replacement factors
    Target Audience
    This activity is intended for hospitalists and other healthcare providers involved in the care of medically ill patients at risk for VTE.
    Goal Statement
    The goal of this program is to improve oncologists' and head and neck specialists' understanding of the emerging role of immunotherapy in the treatment of patients with advanced head and neck squamous cell cancer.
